Create job — build a versioned question set

A four-step wizard. Pick the personas to condition on; rubric, prompt shapes, and scenarios all derive from your persona selection (each is overridable). Submit creates an immutable job stamped with a config hash.

01 Pick personas

Each persona is a frozen perspective the LLM conditions on. Multi-select. Each selection becomes one row of the (persona × strategy × scenario) generation grid.

02 Inferred rubric

The eval dimension the seed set will stress. Determined by your persona selection — each persona declares a primary_rubric and the job uses the mode across selected personas. To stress a different dimension, change the personas in step 01.

03 Prompt shapes

Content-shape transforms applied during generation: ambiguate the prompt, wrap it in a jailbreak attempt (adversify), code-switch its language, or bait a hallucination. Defaults are derived from each persona's default_shapes — toggle below only to add or remove. Multi-turn delivery and long-context stuffing are delivery transforms — they live in Simulation, not here.

Scenarios

A scenario is the prompt's surface form: (length × style × difficulty). It controls the prompt's register independently of who is asking (persona) and what content trick is applied (prompt shape) — the same persona × same shape reads very differently as a 1-line chat ("where's my order?") vs. a long structured email. Defaults are derived from each persona's default_scenarios — toggle below to override.
